01 — Brand Story

What Bashly stands for

Bashly is built for the moments that mark a new chapter — when you step into who you're becoming. We make it effortless for real people to plan, share, and show up to the celebrations that matter most.

Mission

Make milestone celebrations accessible, exciting, and easy to organize — so every teen's Sweet 16 and every grad's big day feels as monumental as it truly is.

Vision

A world where every celebration is as memorable as the milestone itself — where no one has to stress about planning, and every party is a party worth talking about.

Audience

Teens planning their own Sweet 16, parents helping organize it, and young adults celebrating graduation — plus the friends, family, and guests who show up with them.

Positioning

Bashly sits at the intersection of youthful energy and genuine utility — not just a party directory, but a milestone companion that makes the planning as exciting as the event.

02 — Logo & Wordmark

The Bashly wordmark

Set in Bricolage Grotesque — expressive, bold, and highly legible at every size. On light backgrounds, bash uses fern-800 (7.2:1 contrast) and ly uses cerulean-700 (6.6:1). On dark, the electric fern-400 and cerulean-400 are used. Every variation is WCAG AA compliant. Download logo files (wordmark, lockup, icon) in multiple sizes from the Logo Downloader.

Bright Fern

Use fern-500 only for CTA button backgrounds. For any fern-colored text on light surfaces, always use fern-700 or fern-800. Fern-400/500 as text fails contrast on white.

Cerulean

The workhorse of the palette. Cerulean-700+ for text on light. Cerulean-300–400 for text on dark. Cerulean-50–200 for surface backgrounds. Cerulean-500 for large decorative text only.

Supporting

Always use the -700 shade for text on light backgrounds: yellow-700 for achievements, orange-700 for urgency, red-700 for errors. Never convey state through color alone — pair with an icon.

04 — Typography

Type that's readable first

Three typefaces, each chosen for legibility first — personality second. Bricolage Grotesque delivers punch at display sizes while staying clear. Nunito's rounded terminals aid recognition at small body sizes. JetBrains Mono was purpose-built for dense label readability.

Use JetBrains Mono for labels and metadata only — never for body copy. Always allow text to scale up to 200% without loss of content (no overflow: hidden on text containers). Never rely on font style alone to convey meaning.

05 — Voice & Tone

How Bashly talks

Bashly speaks like the most exciting person in the room — not the loudest. Energetic but never try-hard, warm but never cheesy, direct but never cold. Every word should feel like it belongs at the party.

⚡

Energetic

Short sentences. Active verbs. Forward momentum. We bring the hype.

🎯

Direct

No fluff. Say what you mean. Every word earns its place.

🫶

Warm

We actually care about the moment. Celebrations matter. We mean it.

🌟

Celebratory

Everything is a reason to feel good. We amplify joy, never dampen it.

✓ Do say this

"Your crew is waiting. Drop the link."

Short, punchy, action-oriented. Speaks to the user's world.

✕ Not like this

"Please share this event invitation link with your desired attendees at your earliest convenience."

Too formal, too long, sounds like a legal notice.

✓ Do say this

"It's almost time. 3 days to the big one 🎉"

Countdown messaging that builds excitement, not anxiety.

✕ Not like this

"Reminder: Your event is scheduled to occur in 72 hours."

Robotic. Treats a milestone like a calendar alert.

✓ Do say this

"You did it. Now celebrate like you mean it."

Acknowledges the achievement, invites action, feels real.

✕ Not like this

"Congratulations on your achievement. We hope you enjoy using our platform."

Generic corporate congratulations. Could be from any app.

06 — UI Components

Built to celebrate

Every component meets WCAG AA contrast, carries a visible 3px focus ring, and has a minimum 44×44px touch target. Color is never used as the sole indicator of state — every status pairs with a label or icon.
